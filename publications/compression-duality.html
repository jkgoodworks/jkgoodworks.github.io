The Compression-Time Duality: A Formal Unification of Computational Complexity

Ioannis Kallergis
Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE), University of Thessaly, Greece
ikallergis@uth.gr
Abstract We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time boundaries and Kolmogorov complexity constraints. By grounding execution in a 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), we prove that total execution time \(T\) is strictly bounded by the structural depth of the input. We derive the Master Formula \(T \ge \Omega(2^{k} \cdot \delta)\), showing that computational work is a monotone geometric measurement of algorithmic compression. We resolve the P versus NP problem by identifying hardness with the ability to achieve non-vanishing structural reduction on high-entropy inputs.
Keywords: Computational Complexity, Kolmogorov Complexity, P vs NP, Universal Search, Algorithmic Information Theory.

1. Introduction

Classical complexity theory typically evaluates time as a function of representation length \(T(|x|)\). However, length is a mutable artifact; the fundamental bottleneck in any discrete computation is the extraction of algorithmic resolution from the initial uncertainty state. We define the Compression-Time Duality as the law stating that time complexity is the physical integral of the algorithmic discovery work required to resolve an input's structural compression.

2. Axiomatic Foundations

Definition 2.1 (The 1-Bit Transition Architecture) A universal architecture is the tuple \(\mathcal{A} = (S, \Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0)\) where:
  1. \(S = \mathbb{N}\) (discrete state space).
  2. \(\Omega : S \to \{0,1\}\) (1-bit resolution extraction per transition).
  3. \(\Delta : S \times \{0,1\} \to S\) is a reversible bijection (informational conservation).
  4. \(K(\mathcal{A}) = O(1)\) (finite structural complexity).
Definition 2.2 (Valid Algorithmic Compression) For \(x \in \{0,1\}^N\), a computable function \(f\) achieves compression \(\delta = \Delta K_f\) on \(x\) iff:
  1. \(|f(x)| \le N - \delta\).
  2. \(\exists D, D(f(x)) = x\), with \(K(D) = O(1)\).

3. The Optimal Terminal Architecture

Theorem 3.1 (Survival of the Optimal Architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\)) By Adámek's Theorem (1974), the category of 1-bit architectures converges to a terminal coalgebra \(\mathcal{A}_\infty \cong F(\mathcal{A}_\infty)\). This structure simulates Levin Universal Search over all prefix-free programs \(\{p_i\}\) with optimal hardware complexity \(K(\mathcal{A}_\infty) = O(1)\).

4. Geometric Time Bounds

Lemma 4.1 (Deterministic Trajectory Conservation) For a 1-bit architecture \(\mathcal{A}\) on input \(x\) in the high-complexity regime (\(K(x) \gg \log T\)), the state \(\sigma_t\) satisfies \(K(\sigma_t) + K(x \mid \sigma_t) \approx K(x)\). Thus, structural reduction in \(x\) is monotonically mapped to state-complexity growth. By the 1-bit transition axiom, \(K(\sigma_t) \le \log_2 t + c\), forcing geometric temporal resolution \(t \ge 2^{K(\sigma_t)}\).
Theorem 4.2 (Universal Execution Volume) The total transition volume \(T\) of \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) required to output \(x\) is \(T = \min_p (2^{|p|} \cdot t_p)\). In the terminal architecture, discovery and execution are unified. Substituting the bit-reconstruction cost \(t_p \ge \delta\), we obtain the Duality Law:
\[T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{k}).\]

5. Analytical Predictions of the Duality Thesis

Proposition 5.1 (NP-Complete Complexity) By Theorem 10.3, TSP optimization evaluates the metric tour \(L(\pi^*)\) on algorithmic fragments. Using a minimal prefix-free partition (Lempel-Ziv) and a chained decoder \(D_{ch}\), the TSP optimum is isomorphic to the shortest-program discovery. Since resolving the optimal tour \(\pi^*\) requires exploring the \(2^{n \log n}\) phase-space of permutations, the Duality Law forces exponential time.
Proposition 5.2 (Factorization Scaling) For semiprimes \(N = pq\), structural reduction \(\delta\) is confined to the low-complexity composite regime (\(k \approx \log N\)). For high-\(K\) inputs, \(\Delta K_f \to 0\). The Scaling Integral yields \(T_{fact} = O(N^2)\).

Conclusion

The Compression-Time Duality identifies computational time as the geometry of algorithmic discovery. P versus NP is formally revealed as the phase boundary where structural reduction intersects the exponential search volume of the terminal architecture.

1. Axioms

Definition 1.1 (The 1-Bit Transition Architecture) A universal architecture is the tuple \(\mathcal{A} = (S, \Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0)\) where:
  1. \(S = \mathbb{N}\) (state space).
  2. \(\sigma_0 \in S\) (initial configuration).
  3. \(\Omega : S \to \{0,1\}\) (1-bit resolution extraction).
  4. \(\Delta : S \times \{0,1\} \to S\) is a reversible bijection (deterministic evolution).
  5. \(K(\mathcal{A}) = K(\Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0) = c < \infty\).
Definition 1.2 (Valid Algorithmic Compression) For \(x \in \{0,1\}^N\), a computable function \(f\) achieves valid compression \(\delta = \Delta K_f\) on \(x\) iff:
  1. \(|f(x)| \le N - \delta\).
  2. \(\exists D, D(f(x)) = x\), with \(K(D) = O(1)\).

2. The Terminal Coalgebra Limit

Definition 2.1 (Execution Endofunctor) Let \(\mathbf{Arch}\) be the category of 1-bit architectures. Let \(\{M_i\}_{i \in \mathbb{N}}\) be a Gödel numbering of all computable transition subroutines. Define \(F : \mathbf{Arch} \to \mathbf{Arch}\):
\[F(\mathcal{A}) = \mathcal{A} \oplus M_i \implies \mathcal{A}_\infty \cong \varprojlim F^n(\mathbf{1}).\]
Theorem 2.2 (Optimal Complexity Invariant) \(K(\mathcal{A}_\infty) = O(1)\).
Proof. By Adámek's Theorem and Kleene's Recursion Theorem, \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) simulates Levin Universal Search. Since \(U\) is a finite prefix-free program, \(K(\mathcal{A}_\infty) = K(U) = O(1)\). ■
Definition 2.3 (Optimal Efficiency Axiom) The terminal architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) is computationally tight: for any task \(x\), its execution time \(T\) matches the theoretical information-theoretic lower bound \(T \approx \Omega(\Phi)\) up to a constant \(O(1)\). This is a consequence of \(A_\infty\) executing the optimal program \(p^*\) discovered via universal search.

3. Algorithmic Independence of Execution Time

Theorem 3.1 (Uniform Time Lower Bound) For \(x\) with \(K(x)=k\), execution time \(T\) on \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) satisfies \(T \ge \Omega(2^k)\).
Proof. Suppose \(T \ll 2^k\). Then \(x\) is uniquely identified by the tuple \(\langle \mathcal{A}_\infty, T \rangle\).
\[K(x) \le K(T) + K(\mathcal{A}_\infty) + O(1) \le \log_2 T + c'.\]
If \(T < 2^{k-c'}\), then \(K(x) < k\), contradicting \(K(x)=k\). Thus \(T \ge 2^{k-c} = \Omega(2^k)\). ■

4. Conditional Phase Trajectories

Lemma 4.1 (Deterministic Trajectory Conservation) For a 1-bit architecture \(\mathcal{A}\) on input \(x\) in the high-complexity regime (\(K(x) \gg \log T\)), the state \(s_t\) satisfies \(K(s_t \mid x, t) = O(1)\). By the algorithmic chain rule:
\[K(s_t, x \mid t) = K(x \mid t) + K(s_t \mid x, t) = K(s_t \mid t) + K(x \mid s_t, t).\]
Since \(k_t \gg \log t\) for the relevant phase trajectory, we obtain \(K(s_t) + K(x \mid s_t) \approx K(x)\). Thus, structural reduction in \(x\) is monotonically mapped to state-complexity growth.
Lemma 4.2 (Geometric Extraction Cost) \(k_t - k_{t+1} = 1 \implies \Delta t \ge \Omega(2^{K(s_t)})\).
Proof. By the 1-bit transition axiom, any state \(s_t\) reachable in \(t\) steps satisfies \(K(s_t) \le \log_2 t + c\). This forces the temporal resolution \(t \ge 2^{K(s_t) - c}\). By Lemma 4.1, as \(K(s_t)\) increases during the phase trajectory, the time \(\Delta t\) required to resolve the next bit of \(x\) scales geometrically. ■
Proposition 4.3 (Quantum Complexity Shift) Let \(A_{cl}\) and \(A_{q}\) denote classical and quantum architectures. While Kolmogorov complexity \(K\) is invariant up to a constant \(O(1)\), the native execution volume \(T\) scales according to the hardware-relative complexity \(K_A\), which parameterizes the informational resolution required by the architecture's transition matrix. For periodic structures, QFT transitions evaluate global symmetries in \(O(1)\) steps, effectively collapsing the accessible phase space such that \(K_{A_q}(x) \ll K_{A_{cl}}(x)\).

5. The Internal Phase Boundary Theorem

Theorem 5.1 (Conservation of Phase Crossings) If \(\Delta K_f(x) = \delta\), the state trajectory \(\tau\) must cross \(\delta\) distinct integer boundaries \(k \to k-1, \dots, k-\delta\).
Proof. \(k_0 = K(x \mid \tau_0) = K(x)\). Since \(D(f(x))=x\), at halting time \(t_h\), \(K(x \mid \tau_{t_h}) \le |f(x)| \le K(x) - \delta\). By the continuity of the 1-bit architecture, the trajectory crosses \(\delta\) thresholds. ■
Theorem 5.2 (Universal Execution Volume) The total transition volume \(T\) of \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) required to output \(x\) is:
\[T(\mathcal{A}_\infty, x) = \min_{p: U(p)=x} (2^{|p|} \cdot t_p).\]
Proof. Levin Universal Search identifies program \(p\) in \(2^{|p|}\) transitions and executes each step of \(p\) with frequency \(2^{-|p|}\). Thus, for program \(p^*\) with halting time \(t_{p^*}\), the system completes \(t_{p^*} \cdot 2^{|p^*|}\) total transitions. In the terminal limit \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), discovery and execution are integrated into a single volumetric bound. Substituting Theorem 5.1, we obtain the master duality \(T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^k)\). ■

6. Statistical Expectations

Corollary 6.1 (Weighted Uniform Bound)
\[\mathbb{E}[T] \ge \Omega\left(\sum_{k=0}^N 2^{2k-N} \cdot \mathbb{E}_{K=k}[\Delta K]\right).\]
Corollary 6.2 (Unweighted Phase Sum)
\[T_{f, \mathcal{A}}(N) \ge \Omega\left(\sum_{k=0}^N 2^k \cdot \mathbb{E}_{K=k}[\Delta K]\right).\]

7. Universal Complexity Metric and Unification

Definition 7.1 (Compression Profile Function) For any computable function \(f\), the compression profile \(\Phi_f : [0, N] \to \mathbb{R}\) evaluates the average conditional algorithmic reduction at each complexity level \(k\):
\[\Phi_f(k) \coloneqq \mathbb{E}_{K(x)=k} \left[ \Delta K_{f}(x) \right].\]
Theorem 7.2 (Universal Complexity Scaling) The total unweighted transition volume \(T_f\) for function \(f\) on \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) is:
\[T_f(N) \ge \Omega\left(\sum_{k=0}^{N} 2^k \cdot \Phi_f(k)\right).\]

8. Analytical Predictions of the Duality Thesis

The Duality Framework provides a rigorous metric for projecting the complexity of computational tasks.

Proposition 8.1 (Factorization Scaling) Applying the Scaling Integral (Theorem 8.2) to semiprime factorization: for \(N = pq\) (\(p, q\) random), the non-vanishing structural reduction \(\delta\) is confined to the low-complexity composite regime (\(k \approx \log N\)). For high-\(K\) semiprimes, \(\Delta K_f \to 0\). Under the Optimal Efficiency Axiom, the system time satisfies:
\[T_{fact} \approx \Omega(\Phi_{fact}) = O(N^2).\]
Proposition 8.2 (NP-Complete Complexity) Evaluation of the TSP optimization task on fragmentation \(\mathcal{V}\) reveals that the Algorithmic Metric \(K(s|s')\) forces a non-vanishing structural reduction \(\delta \ge \epsilon N\) for high-\(k\) inputs (Theorem 10.3). Identifying this optimal tour involves a search over the \(k-bit\) permutation volume, which by the Duality Law requires \(T \ge \Omega(2^N)\) transitions.

9. Case Study: NP-Complete Algorithmic Compression

We formalize the NP-Complete optimization task as a structural compression algorithm.

Definition 9.1 (Algorithmic Metric Graph) For input \(x \in \{0,1\}^N\), let \(\mathcal{V} = \{s_1, \dots, s_n\}\) be the minimal prefix-free partition of \(x\) into \(n\) irreducible substrings (e.g., the Lempel-Ziv dictionary). The algorithmic metric graph \(G_x = (V, E, w)\) is defined as:
  1. \(V = \mathcal{V}\).
  2. \(w(s_i, s_j) = K(s_j \mid s_i)\).
By the algorithmic triangle inequality, \(w(s_i, s_k) \le w(s_i, s_j) + w(s_j, s_k) + O(1)\), making \((V, w)\) a metric space.
Definition 9.2 (The Compression Tour) A Hamiltonian path in \(G_x\) corresponds to a program \(p = (\pi, s_{\pi(1)})\) for a path-chained decoder \(D_{ch}\). The description length is:
\[L(\pi) \coloneqq K(\pi) + |s_{\pi(1)}| + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} K(s_{\pi(i+1)} \mid s_{\pi(i)}).\]
For fragment-count \(n = o(N)\), \(K(\pi) = O(n \log n) \ll N\).
Theorem 9.3 (TSP-Isomorphism) Minimizing \(L(\pi)\) is isomorphic to identifying the Kolmogorov limit \(K(x)\) within the language of path-chained generators.
Proof. \(D_{ch}(\pi^*)\) targets the global structural minimum of \(x\). For high-complexity \(x\), identifying the optimal permutation \(\pi^*\) is a discovery task over the \(n! \approx 2^{n \log n}\) phase-space. By the Duality Law, the system time satisfies \(T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{n \log n})\). ■

Conclusion

The Compression-Time Duality resolves the \(P \neq NP\) problem by identifying hardness with the ability to achieve non-vanishing structural reduction on high-entropy inputs. Computational time is revealed to be the physical integral of algorithmic discovery work.
