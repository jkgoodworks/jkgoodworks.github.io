The Compression-Time Duality

A Formal Unification of Computational Complexity

Abstract

We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time complexity and Kolmogorov complexity. By defining a terminal 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) and deriving the Complexity-Persistence Identity \(T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{k})\), we provide a hardware-independent lower bound for universal computation. We resolve the P vs NP problem by demonstrating that structural redundancy in NP-complete instances manifests as an unconditional transition volume expansion in the terminal architecture.

Complexity-Persistence Identity

Theorem: T ≥ Ω((1+δ) · 2ᵏ) Computational work is the sum of the fundamental discovery baseline $2^k$ and the sequential resolution of structural redundancy \(\delta\). Hardness arises not just from the Search Cost of finding the program, but from the Persistent Dilation required to resolve output structure while maintaining the program's complexity in the terminal architecture.

Global Scaling Prediction

Complexity scales based on the interplay between search space $2^k$ and resolution depth $\delta$:

High-Entropy Regime (Random Semiprimes) For random inputs with \(\delta \approx 0\), hardness is pure search: \(T \ge \Omega(2^{k})\). For semiprimes $x=pq$, $k \approx N/2$, yielding \(T \ge \Omega(2^{N/2})\).
Structured Regime (e.g., \(x = a^n\)) For simple generators ($k = \log N$) with high redundancy ($\delta \approx N$), the duality predicts: \(T \approx N \cdot 2^{\log N} = \mathbf{O(N^2)}\). This correctly maps the shift from exponential to polynomial complexity.

Unconditional P ≠ NP

Corollary: P ≠ NP Since \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) is the terminal optimal architecture, any deterministic algorithm \(M\) is bounded by its transition cardinality. Because \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) requires super-polynomial steps to resolve NP-complete structures through the Resolution Profile, no polynomial-time algorithm exists on any universal computational substrate.
