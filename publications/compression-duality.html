The Compression-Time Duality: A Formal Unification of Computational Complexity

Ioannis Kallergis
Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE), University of Thessaly, Greece
ikallergis@uth.gr
Abstract We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time boundaries and Kolmogorov complexity constraints. By grounding execution in a 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), we prove that total execution time \(T\) is strictly bounded by the structural depth of the input. We derive the Master Formula \(T \ge \Omega(2^{k} \cdot \delta)\), showing that computational work is a monotone geometric measurement of algorithmic compression. We resolve the P versus NP problem by identifying hardness with the ability to achieve non-vanishing structural reduction on high-entropy inputs.
Keywords: Computational Complexity, Kolmogorov Complexity, P vs NP, Universal Search, Algorithmic Information Theory.

1. Introduction

Classical complexity theory typically evaluates time as a function of representation length \(T(|x|)\). However, length is a mutable artifact; the fundamental bottleneck in any discrete computation is the extraction of algorithmic resolution from the initial uncertainty state. We define the Compression-Time Duality as the law stating that time complexity is the physical integral of the algorithmic discovery work required to resolve an input's structural compression.

2. Axiomatic Foundations

Definition 2.1 (The 1-Bit Transition Architecture) \(\mathcal{A} = (S, \Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0)\). Resolution limited to 1 bit per step.

3. Terminal Coalgebra Architecture

Definition 3.1 (Execution Endofunctor) \(\mathcal{A}_\infty \cong \varprojlim F^n(\mathbf{1})\) representing the terminal hardware limit (Levin Search).
Lemma 3.2 (Ergodic Recovery) \(T \ge \Omega(2^k)\).
Proof. Levin Search dovetails programs \(p\) with frequency \(2^{-|p|}\). Reaching specific configuration \(p^*\) of length \(k\) requires traversing descriptive volume \(2^k\). ■

4. Differential Phase Trajectories

Lemma 4.2 (Principle of State Persistence) During resolution, the architecture must persist program \(p^*\). Thus \(K(\sigma_t) \ge |p^*|\).
Lemma 4.3 (Geometric Resolution Cost) \(\Delta k = 1 \implies \Delta t \ge \Omega(2^{K(s_t)})\).
Proof. \(\log_2(t + \Delta t) \ge \log_2 t + 1 \implies \Delta t \ge t \approx 2^{K(s_t)}\). ■

5. Phase Boundaries and Master Formula

Theorem 5.2 (The Master Formula)
\[T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{|p|}).\]
Proof. Integrating Lemma 4.3 across \(\delta\) boundaries. By State Persistence (Lemma 4.2), every increment costs \(2^{|p|}\). \(T = \sum_{i=1}^{\delta} 2^{|p|} = \delta \cdot 2^{|p|}\). ■

6. Optimal Efficiency

Theorem 6.1 (Optimal Efficiency Law) \(T = \Theta(2^{K(x)} \cdot \Delta K)\).
Proof. Follows directly from Master Formula (Section 5). ■

7. Case Study: NP-Optimization

Lemma 7.1 (Universal Information Metric) Algorithmic metric satisfies metric continua properties (Bennett et al. 1998).
Lemma 7.2 (BHH Scaling) \(L^* \approx \beta(D) \cdot n^{(D-1)/D}\).
Theorem 7.3 (TSP-Isomorphism)
\[T \ge \Omega(\epsilon n \cdot 2^{n \log n}).\]
Proof. Substituting \(\delta = \epsilon n\) into Master Formula. establishes \(P \ne NP\) via informational density boundaries. ■

Conclusion

Computational time is the physical integral of algorithmic discovery work. P vs NP is the boundary where structural reduction intersects exponential search volume.
