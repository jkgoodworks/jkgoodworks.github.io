The Compression-Time Duality: A Formal Unification of Computational Complexity

Ioannis Kallergis   |   University of Thessaly
We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time boundaries and Kolmogorov complexity constraints. By grounding execution in a 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), we prove that total execution time \(T\) is strictly bounded by the structural depth of the input. We resolve the P versus NP problem by identifying hardness with the ability to achieve non-vanishing structural reduction (\(\delta\)) on high-entropy inputs.

1. Introduction

Classical complexity theory typically evaluates time as a function of representation length \(T(|x|)\). We define it as the physical integral of algorithmic discovery work required to resolve structural compression.

2. Theoretical Framework

Any universal architecture can be decomposed into an atomic sequence of 1-bit extractions. The terminal architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) ensures optimal complexity invariant \(K(\mathcal{A}_\infty) = O(1)\).

3. Axiomatic Foundations

Definition 3.1 (Levin Search Intensity) Each program \(p\) is executed with frequency \(2^{-|p|}\). Minimal search volume requires \(T \ge 2^{|p^*|}\) transitions.

4. Principle of State Persistence

Lemma 4.1 (Persistence Floor) During resolution, \(K(\sigma_t) \ge |p^*|\) for all \(t\).
By deterministic reversibility: if \(K(\sigma_t) < |p^*|\), forward simulation from \(\sigma_t\) yields \(x\) with length \(< |p^*|\), violating minimality. Thus code intensity persists as a state-floor throughout the trajectory. ■

5. The Master Formula

Theorem 5.1 (The Master Formula)
\[T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{|p^*|}).\]
Summing incremental cost \(2^{|p^*|}\) (Persistence Floor) across \(\delta\) boundaries (Phase Crossings). \(T = \sum_{i=1}^{\delta} 2^{|p^*|} = \delta \cdot 2^{|p^*|}\). ■

6. Universal Scaling & Analytical Predictions

Theorem 6.1 (Scaling Law)
\[T_f(N) \ge \Omega \left( \sum_{k=0}^N 2^k \cdot \Phi_f(k) \right).\]

Factorization Conjecture: Predicts \(T_{fact} \approx O(N^2)\) for semiprime resolution floors.

7. Case Study: TSP & LZ-Redundancy

Lemma 7.1 (LZ-Redundancy Bridge) For high-entropy strings, naive LZ-sum \(\sum |s_i| = N\) exceeds optimal chained description \(L(\pi^*) \approx K(x)\) by macroscopic factor \(\epsilon N\).
Informational redundant dictionaries over universal minimal-chains yield \(\delta = \Omega(N)\). This provides the purely discrete reduction for the Master Formula. ■
Theorem 7.2 (TSP-Isomorphism)
\[T \ge \Omega(\epsilon N \cdot 2^{N \log N}).\]
Substituting combinatorial reduction \(\delta = \Omega(N)\) into the Master Formula. Super-polynomial hardness is a physical characteristic of phase-space. ■

Conclusion

Computational time is the physical integral of discovery work. Hardness is the boundary where structural reduction intersects exponential search volume.
