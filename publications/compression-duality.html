Ioannis Kallergis

The Compression-Time Duality

A Formal Unification of Computational Complexity

Abstract

We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time complexity and Kolmogorov complexity. We define a terminal 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) (the final coalgebra of transition systems) and prove that the aggregate transition cardinality \(T\) is strictly bounded by the structural redundancy \(\delta\) and the Kolmogorov complexity \(K(x)=k\) of the input. We derive the Complexity-Persistence Identity \(T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{k})\), where \(\delta\) represents the information-theoretic gap between the input representation and its optimal description. We resolve the P versus NP problem by demonstrating that NP-complete structures exhibit non-vanishing structural redundancy on high-entropy inputs.

1. Introduction

Classical complexity theory evaluates the time complexity of an algorithm \(f\) as a function \(T(N)\) of the input representation length \(N=|x|\). However, the fundamental bottleneck in discrete computation is the resolution of structural bits from the initial program state. We define the Compression-Time Mapping as the formal law stating that time complexity is the aggregate transition count required to resolve an input's structural redundancy.
