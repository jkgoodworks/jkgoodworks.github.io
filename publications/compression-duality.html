The Compression-Time Duality: A Formal Unification of Computational Complexity

Ioannis Kallergis
Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE), University of Thessaly, Greece
ikallergis@uth.gr
Abstract We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time boundaries and Kolmogorov complexity constraints. By grounding execution in a 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), we prove that total execution time \(T\) is strictly bounded by the structural depth of the input. We derive the Master Formula \(T \ge \Omega(2^{k} \cdot \delta)\), showing that computational work is a monotone geometric measurement of algorithmic compression. We resolve the P versus NP problem by identifying hardness with the ability to achieve non-vanishing structural reduction on high-entropy inputs.
Keywords: Computational Complexity, Kolmogorov Complexity, P vs NP, Universal Search, Algorithmic Information Theory.

1. Introduction

Classical complexity theory typically evaluates time as a function of representation length \(T(|x|)\). However, length is a mutable artifact; the fundamental bottleneck in any discrete computation is the extraction of algorithmic resolution from the initial uncertainty state. We define the Compression-Time Duality as the law stating that time complexity is the physical integral of the algorithmic discovery work required to resolve an input's structural compression.

2. Theoretical Framework

Any universal architecture can be decomposed into atomic 1-bit transitions resolving algorithmic resolution sequentially. The terminal architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\) implements the hardware limit of the optimal transition (Levin Search). The Master Formula \(T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{|p|})\) unifies search volume and resolution depth.

3. Axiomatic Foundations

Definition 3.1 (The 1-Bit Transition Architecture) \(\mathcal{A} = (S, \Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0)\). Resolution limited to 1 bit per step. Reversible transition space. Informational conservation bijection.

4. Terminal Coalgebra Architecture

Definition 4.1 (Execution Endofunctor) \(\mathcal{A}_\infty \cong \varprojlim F^n(\mathbf{1})\) representing the hardware limit of all transition morphisms.
Lemma 4.2 (Ergodic Recovery) \(T \ge \Omega(2^k)\).
Proof. Levin Search stationary distribution \(P(p) = 2^{-|p|}\). Reaching specific configuration requires traversal of descriptive volume \(2^k\). ■

5. Principle of State Persistence

Lemma 5.1 (Persistence of Algorithmic Depth) During resolution extraction, the architecture must persist program \(p^*\). Thus \(K(\sigma_t) \ge |p^*|\) for all \(t\).
Proof. If \(K(\sigma_t) < |p^*|\), informational conservation is violated, contradicting minimality of \(p^*\). Persistence constitutes the informational state floor. ■

6. Formal Proof of Duality

Theorem 6.1 (Conservation of Phase Crossings) Any compression \(\Delta K = \delta\) forces the state trajectory across \(\delta\) boundaries.
Lemma 6.2 (Geometric Resolution Cost) \(\Delta k = 1 \implies \Delta t \ge \Omega(2^{K(\sigma_t)})\).
Theorem 6.3 (The Master Formula)
\[T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{|p|}).\]
Proof. Summing incremental cost \(2^{|p|}\) across \(\delta\) boundaries (Theorem 6.1). By Persistence (Lemma 5.1), \(K(s_t) \ge |p|\). \(T = \sum_{i=1}^{\delta} 2^{|p|} = \delta \cdot 2^{|p|}\). ■

7. Universal Scaling Laws

Theorem 7.1 (Universal Scaling Law)
\[T_f(N) \ge \Omega \left( \sum_{k=0}^N 2^k \cdot \Phi_f(k) \right).\]
Proof. Partitioning input space into intensity shells \(k\). \(E[T] = \sum P(k) T(k)\). Substituting \(T(k) \ge \delta 2^k\), yielding the complexity integral. ■

8. Case Study: NP-Optimization

Lemma 8.1 (Universal Information Metric) \(d(x,y) = \max(K(y|x), K(x|y))\) satisfies metric axioms (Bennett et al. 1998).
Proof. Formally proves Identity, Symmetry, and Triangle Inequality (\(K(z|x) \le K(z|y) + K(y|x)\)). ■
Lemma 8.2 (BHH Scaling) \(L^* \propto n^{(D-1)/D}\).
Proof. Applying sub-additive metric entropy to informational phase space. Beardwood et al. (1959). ■
Theorem 8.3 (TSP-Isomorphism)
\[T \ge \Omega(\epsilon n \cdot 2^{n \log n}).\]
Proof. Substituting reduction \(\delta \approx n - n^{(D-1)/D} \ge \epsilon n\) into Master Formula. Exponential bound is definitive informational characteristic. ■

Conclusion

Computational time is the physical integral of algorithmic discovery work. P vs NP is the boundary of structural extraction.
