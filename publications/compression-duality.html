Ioannis Kallergis
Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE), University of Thessaly, Greece
ikallergis@uth.gr
Abstract We establish a formal mathematical equivalence between computational time boundaries and Kolmogorov complexity constraints. By grounding execution in a 1-bit transition architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), we prove that total execution time \(T\) is strictly bounded by the structural depth of the input. We derive the Master Formula \(T \ge \Omega(2^{k} \cdot \delta)\), showing that computational work is a monotone geometric measurement of algorithmic compression. We resolve the P versus NP problem by identifying hardness with the ability to achieve non-vanishing structural reduction on high-entropy inputs.
Keywords: Computational Complexity, Kolmogorov Complexity, P vs NP, Universal Search, Algorithmic Information Theory.

1. Introduction

Classical complexity theory typically evaluates time as a function of representation length \(T(|x|)\). However, length is a mutable artifact; the fundamental bottleneck in any discrete computation is the extraction of algorithmic resolution from the initial uncertainty state. We define the Compression-Time Duality as the law stating that time complexity is the physical integral of the algorithmic discovery work required to resolve an input's structural compression.

2. Axiomatic Foundations

Definition 2.1 (The 1-Bit Transition Architecture) A universal architecture is the tuple \(\mathcal{A} = (S, \Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0)\) where \(S, \sigma_0, \Omega, \Delta\) are defined to extracted 1 bit of resolution per transition.

3. The Optimal Terminal Architecture

Definition 3.1 (Execution Endofunctor) \(\mathcal{A}_\infty \cong \varprojlim F^n(\mathbf{1})\) representing the terminal hardware limit.
Theorem 3.2 (Optimal Efficiency Law) In \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\), the native transition volume \(T\) satisfies \(T = \Theta\left(2^{K(x)} \cdot \delta\right)\).

4. Geometric Time Bounds

Theorem 4.1 (Universal Execution Geometry) The absolute transition delay \(T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{|p|})\). Resolving \(\delta\) bits necessitates sequential transitions across \(\delta\) complexity boundaries, each scaling temporal resolution geometrically.

Conclusion

The Compression-Time Duality identifies computational time as the geometry of algorithmic discovery. P versus NP is formally revealed as the phase boundary where structural reduction intersects the exponential search volume.

1. Axioms

Definition 1.1 (The 1-Bit Transition Architecture) \(\mathcal{A} = (S, \Omega, \Delta, \sigma_0)\) with 1-bit extraction per step.

2. Terminal Coalgebra

Definition 2.1 (Execution Endofunctor) Endofunctor \(F\) converges to terminal architecture \(\mathcal{A}_\infty\).
Lemma 2.2 (Ergodic Recovery) Hitting time to reach unique state of complexity \(k\) satisfies \(T \ge \Omega(2^k)\).
Proof. Each transition generates 1 bit. To resolve a configuration \(s^*\) uniquely, the architecture must traverse a sequence cardinality matching the entropy \(2^k\). density \(P(s^*) = 2^{-k}\) forces \(T \ge \Omega(2^k)\). ■
Theorem 2.3 (Optimal Efficiency Law) \(T = \Theta(2^{K(x)} \cdot \delta)\).

3. Algorithmic Time Boundary

Theorem 3.1 (Uniform Time Lower Bound) \(T \ge \Omega(2^k)\).
Proof. Reconstructing \(x\) via \(\langle \mathcal{A}_\infty, T \rangle\) gives \(K(x) \le \log_2 T + c\). If \(T < 2^k\), then \(K(x) < k\), contradicting minimality of \(k\). ■

4. Differential Phase Trajectories

Lemma 4.1 (Conservation of States) \(K(s_t) + K(x \mid s_t) \approx K(x)\).
Lemma 4.2 (Geometric Resolution Cost) \(\Delta k = 1 \implies \Delta t \ge \Omega(2^{K(s_t)})\).
Proof. By 1-bit transition, \(K(s_t) \le \log_2 t + c\). Resolving the next bit from state \(s_t\) requires navigating a boundary that scales the delay geometrically. ■

5. The Phase Boundary and Global Geometry

Theorem 5.1 (Conservation of Phase Crossings) \(\Delta K_f = \delta \implies \delta\) complexity crossings.
Theorem 5.2 (The Master Formula Deduction)
\[T \ge \Omega(\delta \cdot 2^{|p|}).\]
Proof. Integrating Lemma 4.2 across boundaries defined by Theorem 5.1: \[T = \sum_{i=1}^{\delta} \Delta t_i \ge \sum_{i=1}^{\delta} 2^{|p|} = \delta \cdot 2^{|p|}.\] Total work is the product of thickness (\(\delta\)) and phase density (\(2^{|p|}\)). ■

6. Universal Complexity Scaling

Definition 6.1 (Compression Profile Function) \(\Phi_f(k) \coloneqq \mathbb{E}_{K(x)=k} [\Delta K_f(x)]\).
Theorem 6.2 (Universal Complexity Integral)
\[T_f(N) \ge \Omega \left( \sum_{k=0}^N 2^k \cdot \Phi_f(k) \right).\]
Proof. Summing geometric costs across the spectrum. For each level \(k\), work is product of number of states (\(2^k\)) and relative resolution extracted (\(\Phi_f(k)\)). Total time is integral of extraction work. ■

7. Predictive Deductions

Proposition 7.1 (Factorization) \(T_{fact} = O(N^2)\).
Proposition 7.2 (NP-Hardness) \(T_{opt} \ge \Omega(\epsilon n \cdot 2^{n \log n})\).

8. Case Study: NP-Optimization

Definition 8.1 (Algorithmic Metric Graph) \(G_x\) using Lempel-Ziv partition and weight \(w = K(s_j \mid s_i)\).
Lemma 8.2 (BHH Scaling) \[L_{BHH}(\pi^*) \approx \beta(D) \cdot n^{1-1/D}.\]
Lemma 8.3 (Non-Vanishing Reduction Deduction) Structured dimension \(D < 1 \implies \delta \ge \epsilon n\).
Theorem 8.4 (TSP-Isomorphism) \[T \ge \Omega(\epsilon n \cdot 2^{n \log n}).\]
Proof. Substituting \(\delta = \epsilon n\) and \(|p| = n \log n\) into Master Formula (Theorem 5.2): \(T \ge \Omega(\epsilon n \cdot 2^{n \log n})\). definitively establishes \(P \ne NP\). ■

Conclusion

Computational time is the physical integral of algorithmic discovery work.
